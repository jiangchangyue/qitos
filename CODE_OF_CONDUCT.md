# Code Of Conduct

## Our Standard

QitOS is committed to a respectful, inclusive, and constructive community.

Participants are expected to:

- be respectful in language and tone
- assume good intent while giving direct technical feedback
- focus criticism on ideas, code, and behavior, not people
- welcome contributors at different experience levels
- avoid harassment, discrimination, and intimidation

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- personal attacks, insults, or trolling
- discriminatory or sexualized language
- repeated bad-faith disruption of discussions or reviews
- publishing private information without permission

## Reporting

If you experience or observe unacceptable behavior:

- use GitHub's private reporting features when available
- otherwise contact the repository maintainers directly through GitHub
- avoid filing a public issue for sensitive conduct reports

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove comments, close discussions, reject contributions, or ban participants when needed to protect the community.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to project spaces, issues, pull requests, discussions, review threads, and community interactions related to QitOS.
